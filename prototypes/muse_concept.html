The Old House

The house stood on a slight rise just on the edge of the village. It stood on its own and looked over a broad spread of West Country farmland. Not a remarkable house by any means—it was about thirty years old, squattish, squarish, made of brick, and had four windows set in the front of a size and proportion which gave it an unpleasant expression.

It was the kind of house that seemed to be watching you, waiting for you to make a mistake.
